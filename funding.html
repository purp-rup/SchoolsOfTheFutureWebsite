Funding

Free Community School Education Grant

At the Philadelphia Community Free School our main base of funding is through the government. Since Title 1 has been dismantled due to the budget cuts we rely solely on specialized grants from the government. Every year our school applies for the Free Community School Education Grant (FCSEG). This is a specialized grant only offered to schools who facilitate in “free” community-based environment and nonprofit organizations. This grant is only available for schools operating up to eighth grade.

With this grant we are able to receive up to one million dollars every five years. With these funds we are able to provide our wellness center, boarding for fostered and homeless children, and all our supplies we provide for our staff. We invest this money in the development of our specialized community-based curriculum. We invest in our dedication to hiring our diverse staff to reflect the local population, and lastly in our dedicated nurses, doctors, and social workers.

Some specialized programs we offer are mental health workshops that we offer inside and outside of the school, we love to promote family engagement. Other services we use our funding for are STEM programs and specialized life skills programs. Some of these life skill programs include cooking, building, and financial literacy classes. We believe these courses help set up our students for life success outside of the classroom.

In addition to the grant we receive, we host many fundraisers and community events to help raise money for our school. We encourage our students, their families, and our staff to participate in these events to help promote our community-based structure. Some of the examples we do are bake sales, selling stuff the students have made in class, and inviting vendors to come and donate some of their proceeds to our school.

We also take in donations such as clothes, personal hygiene supplies, and food. Since we offer housing for our foster and homeless children, we appreciate anything that can be contributed. We have a pantry at our school so that items can be dropped off. Any items that you would like to be donated can be dropped off during school hours (8am–4pm). We accept items at all fundraisers and events also.

I would like to note that all proceeds, donations, and money made during fundraising goes directly to our school. We use one hundred percent of our profits to benefit the students and help make our school a better place for all that are there. We would like to thank everyone for their participation in any event we have held or donations you may have offered, they help our students more than you know.

Julia Clark
