PCFS Philosophy

What We Believe In

At PCFS we focus on a holistic approach to learning and development with our K-8 students. With the collapse of the past education systems, we find that incorporating a supportive learning environment is crucial to supporting child development in this time of transition.

Our 6 core values at PCFS are diversity, safety, freedom, equality, inclusion and opportunity.

  • Diversity: We believe that immersing students in other cultures and socioeconomic backgrounds grants students a type of intellect other schools may lack in providing.

  • Safety: Providing students with a safe environment to play, explore and learn is key in healthy childhood development.

  • Freedom: Allowing children autonomy to make their own decisions regarding their education, can promote passionate learning.

  • Equality: All students have the same freedoms and access to support and education through our school.

  • Inclusion: Our curriculum promotes inclusive learning environments that align with the students educational needs and goals.

  • Opportunity: PCFS provides students with opportunities beyond learning. All students have the opportunity to apply for healthcare, room and board, and meal plans provided through the school.

We offer community based support and learning opportunities that allow students to get to know their neighboring communities and cultures. Our belief is that a sense of belonging and strong community building can help develop multiple types of intelligences that are not normally focused on in traditional school settings. At PCFS our staff and students regularly host community events such as clothes drives, food drives, and toy drives that directly go to the students within the school.

What makes PCFS so special is the diversity in our student population. Children from all cultural, religious, racial and socioeconomic backgrounds are included in our school. The intention behind this is to expose students to a sense of understanding, open-mindedness and acceptance of those different from themselves in early development. Our hope is that this experience in early development will better equip students with sociocultural intelligence that other schools may lack in providing.

We believe that students have the right to pursue the education route they want. While focusing on basic educational and life skills for every student, we encourage the students to take classes geared around their interests and what they would like to learn more of. Though this is traditionally an approach used in higher education, we believe that this can promote children to learn more passionately and intently.
