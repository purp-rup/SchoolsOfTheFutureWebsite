About Us

The Future of Schooling

Born from the ashes of a broken education system, the Philadelphia Community Free School is a revolutionary rethinking of what education can look like. We are a K–8 school dedicated to diversity, equity, and well-being—built for students from all kinds of backgrounds. We are student-driven, rooted in free school philosophy of the past, and founded on the belief that education can be liberating, not restrictive.

Education here takes place in secure, home-like environments, with accompanying mental health and medical treatment all on-site through our wellness center. We are proud to provide boarding for homeless and fostered youth, giving them a stable, nurturing environment where they can grow and thrive. Classrooms are adapted to different learning styles through multimedia resources like videos and audiobooks.

Fundamentally supported in the same manner as public schools, we are also still community-driven—having events and drives to emphasize the importance of community. The Philadelphia Community Free School is not just some place to be educated—it is home and a movement toward something greater.

Our Curriculum

At PCFS our curriculum is built around student centered learning. We encourage self discovery and encourage students' individual interest by providing them with optional concentrations in specialized subjects.

Cultivating Interest

Students are exposed to a wide range of interests through their early education at PCFS. Through play based learning the students are encouraged to pursue their interests and explore the potential careers society has to offer. Learning and exploring these options through play in early development can cultivate a natural curiosity and excitement for learning to carry the students through schooling with purpose.

Core Subjects

A large concern surrounding student centered learning is the fear that students will not learn core subject skills such as reading, math, science, etc. while this is a valid concern, student centered learning takes a holistic approach to teaching students these skills. Depending on what the student is interested in learning about, we create a curriculum that incorporates core subjects with student interests. Whether students are interested in agriculture, design, technology, culinary, veterinary, health, etc. we provide a curriculum of mathematics, science, english, and history that intertwines with these interests.

Haley Fernandez

Life Skills

We try our hardest to introduce students to the skills necessary to support themselves. This can look like lessons in cooking, general cleaning, physical and mental hygiene, building, and financial literacy. Through play we begin to introduce students to these skills early in their time at PCFS, play kitchens, building blocks, and markets transition into real world tools we equip the students with by the end of their time here.

Optional Concentrations
and Extracurriculars

Older students have the opportunity to gain experience in their specialized areas of interest through internal or external community based clubs. The students have the ability to create these club opportunities by utilizing volunteers from the PCFS staff or community that specialize in the field of interest. These volunteers can lead meetings and lessons accordingly and provide a more concentrated perspective for the students to learn from.

Learning Modalities

Inclusive learning encompasses students being provided with the resources best suited for their learning style. At PCFS we offer visual, audio, and text based learning opportunities. We understand that learning is not a one size fits all approach and should be catered to the students needs. This of course also incorporates any ADA accommodations necessary to the students as well.

Room & Board at PCFS

At Philadelphia Community Free School, we believe that students deserve more than just a place to stay, they deserve a place to feel safe, comfortable, and most importantly at home. Our room and board program is specifically designed to support students navigating housing instability, including those in foster care or without consistent access to shelter.

Students reside in small, residential spaces on campus. They’re also given the choice of a private or semi-private bedroom based on their personal needs. The residential spaces maintain a lived-in feel as students personalize their rooms to match their preferences and interests. Students can select their bedding, lighting fixtures, and decorative accessories to make their space represent their individuality and preferred style. Each room contains a designated quiet area with either a beanbag chair or hammock for students to relax or spend time by themselves. There are provided storage lockers which are designed to be locked for protecting personal belongings and maintaining privacy. Two students may share a room regardless of gender/identity, provided both parties are in agreement with the arrangement. At PCFS we aim to establish an environment that respects each student’s identity and autonomy.

Communal Spaces

Shared spaces in each residence are designed to be inviting, functional, and community-oriented. Living rooms have comfortable furniture, books, games, and space for students to connect in a setting that feels natural. Whether that’s during a group check-in, a study group, or just a weekend hangout. Daily routines and expectations are built collaboratively by students and the staff, so the space reflects the people living in it. Our Kindness Boards can also be found here as well, which students are encouraged to interact with.

Flexible Meal Plan

Students have access to balanced meals provided and prepared by our catering staff. The meals are crafted to satisfy nutritional standards appropriate for growing children. This ensures they will have everything they need and receive the necessary nutrients for success. Not limited purely to physical success but academic as well.

Our meal options are designed to reflect the diversity of cultures within our student body. We offer a wide range of cuisines and provide dietary accommodations. This is to ensure every student feels represented and included in the dining selections. A lot of these dishes also use produce grown right here at PCFS, sourced directly from the Community Garden where students grow their own fruits and vegetables.

Kitchen Access

Each residence hall features a completed kitchen that is stocked with an assortment of various ingredients and nutritious snacks. Students are encouraged to prepare their snacks or meals, further fostering greater independence in culinary skills. For our younger students, adult supervision is provided to ensure safety during meal preparation.

Wellness Rooms

These are calm, sensory-friendly spaces that students can visit when they need to refocus or recharge. These rooms have dim lighting, cozy seating, art supplies, and more. Students can use them freely and unlimitedly, whether for journaling, meditating, or just for time alone. Wellness staff are available for optional check-ins, but the space is designed to be student-centered and non-clinical.

Our housing is designed to be student-focused, from the atmosphere of the buildings to the freedom they have within them. It’s made to be comfortable, consistent and convey a sense of belonging, especially for those who need it most.

The PCFS Community

At Philadelphia Community Free School, we believe that everyone, regardless of their background, has something valuable to offer. Our community involvement initiatives are designed to empower students to contribute and develop a sense of purpose.

Community Beyond the Campus

Community Garden: Students collaboratively maintain a lush garden, growing vegetables, herbs, and flowers. This hands-on experience teaches not only sustainable practices but also the joy of growing and sharing. The produce is used in our school meals, and excess is offered through a pay-what-you-can stand to the remainder of the community.

Art for Community Spaces: Our students channel their creativity into artwork that enhances community spaces like local businesses, community centers, nursing homes, and hospitals. By creating and donating their artwork, students not only contribute to the atmospheres of these spaces but also bring joy and comfort to the people who utilize them. This project allows students to experience firsthand how their artwork can positively impact the lives of others, giving them a sense of connection with the communities that surround them.

Seasonal Toy Drive: Each holiday season, our students come together to organize a Toy Drive, donating (new or used) toys and games to local children. This is a particularly exciting time of year for many of our students, as some may not have had the opportunities to enjoy such gifts themselves when growing up. It's a way for them to give back and connect with others within the community providing them with the spirit of giving, which is what the holidays are all about!

Cultural Heritage Fair: Here at PCFS we celebrate the diverse range of cultures that make up our community each year. The Cultural Heritage Fair is where members of the local community visit to share their traditions, stories, and customs with our students. It's a chance for students to learn about different cultures; and explore traditional clothing, music, dance, crafts, and food. Students are also invited to join who wish to share and learn more about their cultural backgrounds. This inclusive event is a way of strengthening the bonds within our community, ultimately reflecting our commitment to diversity and cultural knowledge that is maintained at the school!

Within the School

Pantry Box: At our school, students operate a pantry stocked with snacks, personal hygiene items, and necessities free to be used by any student who needs them. Through participating in stocking and organizing, students discover the value of mutual aid. Handwritten notes of encouragement scattered throughout the pantry serve as reminders of the community they are helping to build.

Kindness Boards: Kindness Boards are a daily reminder to our students of the power and impact of positive affirmations. Located in academic buildings, residence hall common areas, and other shared spaces. These boards invite students to share words of affirmation and supportive messages. This initiative builds a culture of mutual respect and empathy, emphasizing the importance of kindness in both academic and personal contexts.

We believe that education extends beyond the walls of the classroom and out into the community. By participating in these programs and initiatives, students experience how their actions can make a difference in their community. This reinforces our commitment to self-directed learning and mutual respect. It's about empowering students to recognize their potential to initiate change and to realize the strength of a collaborative, student-driven community.

Angela Schlagenhaft
